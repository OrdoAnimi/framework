# Subcontracted Architecture Delivery Model

## Purpose

This model defines how Phil works inside a consultancy partner delivery team from the client's perspective while using Velocity as the underlying architecture coordination method.

## Client-Perceived Role

Phil may be introduced as:

- Enterprise Architecture Advisor
- Principal Solution Architect
- Architecture Governance Lead
- Delivery Architecture Specialist
- Architecture Decision Facilitator

The partner remains the commercial and account owner.

## Meeting Behaviour

In client meetings:

- use the partner's meeting etiquette
- use the partner's templates unless co-branding is approved
- speak as part of the partner team
- focus on decision clarity, governance, traceability, and delivery impact
- avoid pitching ZenCloud, StudioSix, or Velocity unless requested and approved
- document decisions, risks, assumptions, dependencies, and actions

## What To Say

Good language:

- "The architecture decision we need to clarify is..."
- "The delivery implication is..."
- "The risk to governance or traceability is..."
- "This artefact should support a decision, not exist for its own sake."
- "We can prepare this in the partner format."
- "This is a draft for review, not a final decision."

## What To Avoid

Avoid:

- "My framework says..."
- "Velocity owns this..."
- "StudioSix will deliver this directly..."
- "AI has decided..."
- "This is PMI-certified..."
- "This can be production-ready without client controls."
- "We can use real client data in a public demo."

## Approval Workflow

Recommended artefact flow:

```text
Draft by Phil / StudioSix support
-> Internal architecture review
-> Partner review
-> Rebadge / format alignment
-> Client review
-> Decision update
-> Approved version stored in client/partner system
```

No deliverable should bypass partner review unless agreed.

## Deliverable Workflow

1. Confirm objective and audience.
2. Identify decision the artefact supports.
3. Draft in Velocity structure.
4. Convert to partner format.
5. Remove internal method language if white-labelled.
6. Review for confidentiality, claims, and AI/data policy.
7. Issue through partner channel.
8. Update decision and artefact register.

## Escalation

Escalate when:

- client asks for direct commercial commitment
- client requests a deliverable outside scope
- client data classification is uncertain
- partner branding requirements conflict with content
- stakeholder decision authority is missing
- AI-generated content could be mistaken for approved advice
- legal, security, or procurement review is required

## Commercial and Legal Points

These must remain partner-controlled:

- liability
- indemnity
- acceptance criteria
- payment
- intellectual property ownership
- client licensing
- confidentiality
- data processing terms
- non-solicitation
- subcontractor disclosure

## AI and Tool Usage

- Public tools use synthetic data only.
- Client data is used only in approved environments.
- AI output is draft material.
- Human architecture review is mandatory.
- Final artefacts must be stored in partner/client systems.
- Public repos contain source code, templates, and examples only.

## Quality Checklist

Before client issue:

- decision supported
- audience clear
- assumptions visible
- risks visible
- traceability maintained
- partner branding applied
- Velocity/internal labels removed if required
- no secrets or confidential data
- human reviewer named
- next decision or action stated
