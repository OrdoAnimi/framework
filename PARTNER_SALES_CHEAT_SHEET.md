# Partner Sales Cheat Sheet

## One-Line Positioning

Velocity helps organisations move from architecture documents to faster, clearer, traceable enterprise decisions.

## Buyer Personas

- CEO / COO: wants execution confidence and fewer surprises.
- CIO / CTO: wants architecture governance that does not slow delivery.
- Transformation executive: wants programme alignment and decision visibility.
- PMO leader: wants delivery control, RAID visibility, and governance cadence.
- Enterprise architect: wants influence, traceability, and business value.
- Solution architect: wants clear guardrails, decisions, and reusable artefacts.

## Trigger Phrases

Listen for:

- "We have too many documents but still slow decisions."
- "Architecture is seen as a blocker."
- "The PMO and architecture teams are disconnected."
- "We cannot explain why decisions were made."
- "Executives do not trust the status reports."
- "AI is being used, but governance is unclear."
- "We need architecture discipline without another platform."

## Pain Signals

- unclear decision rights
- weak artefact reuse
- architecture review bottlenecks
- inconsistent solution documentation
- PMO and EA conflict
- no traceability from strategy to delivery
- executive reporting lacks decision context
- data/security/AI concerns appear too late

## Discovery Questions

1. Which decisions are slowest or most contested?
2. Which artefacts are produced but not used?
3. Who owns architecture decisions today?
4. What happens when PMO and architecture disagree?
5. How are risks, assumptions, and decisions traced?
6. Where does AI already appear in delivery?
7. What would executives need to see weekly?
8. What must be private, rebadged, or partner-controlled?

## Offer Fit

| Pain | Offer |
|---|---|
| Executives need a fast view of architecture risk | Executive Architecture FastStart |
| Delivery is blocked by unclear decisions | Architecture Decision Sprint |
| Solution architecture practice is inconsistent | VAF-SA Engagement Diagnostic |
| PMO and architecture are disconnected | PMO Governance Alignment |
| AI adoption lacks controls | Velocity AI Enablement |
| Data/security readiness is unclear | Data/Security readiness assessment |

## What Not To Promise

- Do not promise production-ready tooling without hardening.
- Do not promise AI will make final decisions.
- Do not promise confidential data can go into public tools.
- Do not promise fixed outcomes before discovery.
- Do not promise Velocity must be client-visible in white-label mode.
- Do not promise legal, financial, or regulated compliance sign-off.

## Safe Close

"The best next step is a short architecture decision discovery. We identify the decision-flow problem, map the stakeholders and artefacts, and recommend whether a FastStart, Decision Sprint, or PMO alignment engagement is warranted."
