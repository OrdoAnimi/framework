**ZenCloud Global Consultants**  
Classification: Commercial in Confidence

## Engagement Context

ZenCloud operates as an architecture advisory practice built on the Velocity Architecture Framework. Where a business consultancy partner leads enterprise client relationships, ZenCloud provides the architecture branch — decision-first operating model, AI-assisted artefact production, and practitioner-led delivery.

This document defines how that partnership works operationally.

---

## Role of the Consultancy Partner

The partner:

- Owns the client relationship
- Defines the engagement scope and commercial terms with the client
- Introduces ZenCloud / Velocity capability as part of their architecture service offering
- Coordinates with ZenCloud on engagement governance and milestone reporting
- Manages client stakeholder expectations and escalation
- Retains commercial accountability for the engagement

---

## Role of ZenCloud / Phil

ZenCloud:

- Provides architecture advisory capability under the Velocity Architecture Framework
- Delivers decision-first architecture engagements (see Engagement Models below)
- Produces all architecture artefacts using VAF operating model and Agentic AI tooling
- Maintains practitioner accountability for every output delivered
- Advises on AI readiness, data governance, security architecture, and enterprise design
- Reports engagement progress to the partner per agreed cadence

Phil Myint operates as Principal Architect and lead practitioner on ZenCloud engagements.

---

## Role of StudioSix

StudioSix is ZenCloud's product, media, and publishing arm.

Within a partner engagement, StudioSix may provide:

- Presentation design and client-ready deck production
- Research paper and white paper production
- Training module and certification content production
- Recording, editing, and media packaging for client-facing content
- Framework publishing and documentation design

StudioSix does not engage directly with enterprise clients. It operates as a production layer behind ZenCloud advisory.

---

## Role of Velocity Architecture Framework

Velocity is the method layer.

It provides:

- Decision-first operating model used in every engagement
- Four-box architecture model (EA / BA / SA / PMO)
- Cross-cutting lenses (Business Value, Data, Security, AI Readiness, Governance)
- Certified artefact set (Board Pack, ADR, Trade-off Matrix, Decision Register, Risk Register)
- VAF Agentic AI tooling for artefact production
- Certification pathway for client practitioners (if applicable)

Velocity is not a brand presented to clients as a separate entity. It is the operating model ZenCloud deploys in advisory engagements. How it is named or presented to the end client is determined by the white-label vs named delivery decision (see below).

---

## White-Label vs Named Delivery

### White-Label

The partner presents ZenCloud advisory capability as part of their own architecture practice.

- ZenCloud is not named in client-facing materials
- Velocity methodology is applied but branded under the partner's framework
- Artefacts carry the partner's document standards and branding
- Phil operates as a specialist resource introduced by the partner practice

Applicable when: the partner has an established architecture brand and wishes to augment it.

### Named Delivery

ZenCloud and/or Velocity are named as the advisory partner in client-facing materials.

- ZenCloud is introduced as a specialist architecture advisory firm
- Velocity Architecture Framework is named as the operating model
- VAF Agentic AI may be referenced as the tool layer
- Co-branding with the consultancy partner is agreed in advance

Applicable when: the client values transparency about the advisory model, or when Velocity certification is part of the engagement offering.

The naming preference is agreed per engagement. Default is white-label unless otherwise specified.

## Delivery Responsibilities

ZenCloud commits to:

- Structured engagement kick-off within agreed timeframe
- Clear definition of decision questions before work commences
- Regular delivery cadence per engagement model (see below)
- Practitioner review of all AI-generated outputs before delivery
- Board-ready artefacts at agreed milestone points
- Proactive identification of scope risks and blockers
- Engagement close-out report with decision register summary

ZenCloud does not commit to:

- On-site delivery unless separately agreed
- Client system access beyond that required for the engagement
- Production implementation of any design recommendation
- Staffing continuity beyond Phil as lead practitioner without separate agreement

## IP Boundaries

### Velocity Framework IP

All Velocity Architecture Framework methods, templates, operating models, lenses, artefact standards, and certification content are ZenCloud / Velocity IP.

The partner may use these methods in engagements delivered with ZenCloud. The partner may not independently reproduce, sell, or redistribute Velocity Framework materials without a separate licensing agreement.

### Client IP

All engagement outputs produced for a specific client (Board Packs, ADRs, Decision Registers, Risk Registers, Assumptions Registers, program-specific Trade-off Matrices) are client IP upon delivery, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

### Partner IP

Partner-contributed methods, frameworks, templates, or client context remain partner IP. ZenCloud does not claim ownership of partner assets.

### Generated Deliverables

AI-assisted artefacts produced during an engagement are treated as client IP upon practitioner review and delivery. The fact that AI tooling was used in production does not transfer any IP claim to the AI provider. Refer to `AI_AND_CLIENT_DATA_USAGE_POLICY.md` for full treatment.

## Confidentiality Principles

- Engagement details are not shared outside the delivery team without client and partner consent
- Client data and context are handled per the AI and Data Usage Policy
- ZenCloud does not use client engagement experience to produce publicly attributed case studies without explicit written consent
- Aggregated, anonymised, and generalised insights from engagements may be used to improve the Velocity Framework

---

## AI and Tool Usage Rules

- All AI-generated content is reviewed by the practitioner before delivery
- No confidential client data enters any public AI system
- VAF Agentic AI operates on sanitised or synthetic data during engagement scoping
- Client-approved data used in engagement tooling is governed per the AI and Data Usage Policy
- The partner is informed of tool usage at engagement kick-off

## Delivery Cadence

Standard engagement cadence:

- **Week 1:** Kick-off, decision question definition, scope confirmation
- **Weekly:** Short sync with partner lead (30 minutes)
- **Fortnightly:** Artefact review checkpoint
- **Milestone:** Board pack or decision register delivery at agreed point
- **Close-out:** Engagement summary, decision register handover, lessons captured

Cadence is adapted per engagement model.

## Example Engagement Models

### Advisory Overlay

ZenCloud provides architecture advisory alongside an existing client delivery team.

- Practitioner attends key architecture forums and governance gates
- Reviews and challenges design decisions using the Velocity framework
- Produces decision records and governance artefacts on behalf of the architecture function
- Does not own delivery — advises the team that does

Typical duration: 3–12 months rolling

### Embedded Architecture Advisor

Phil operates within a client program as an embedded architecture practitioner.

- Attends program governance, architecture review, and PMO checkpoints
- Acts as the architecture decision authority for the program domain
- Produces all architecture artefacts for the program using VAF tools
- Reports to the program director and architecture board

Typical duration: 1–6 months per program

### Decision Sprint

A bounded, time-boxed engagement focused on a single architecture decision question.

- Week 1: Problem framing, options identification
- Week 2: Options analysis using cross-cutting lenses
- Week 3: Trade-off matrix and recommendation
- Week 4: Board pack production and review
- Output: Board Pack, ADR, Decision Register entry

Typical duration: 4 weeks

### Capability Uplift

ZenCloud delivers Velocity certification and training to a client or partner team.

- Foundation certification: 2-day workshop + assessment
- Practitioner certification: 4-day workshop + supervised engagement deliverable
- Customised delivery available for partner cohorts

Typical duration: 2–5 days per cohort

### Architecture Governance Reset

An enterprise architecture function is producing documentation but not delivering governance value. ZenCloud applies the Velocity decision-first model to reset the operating model.

- Current-state diagnostic using Decision Maturity Assessment
- Operating model redesign using four-box model
- ADR process implementation
- Board pack format standardisation
- Practitioner uplift for internal EA team

Typical duration: 6–12 weeks
