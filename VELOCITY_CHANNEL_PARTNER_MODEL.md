# Velocity Channel Partner Model

## Purpose

This model defines partner pathways for taking Velocity, ZenCloud, and StudioSix capabilities to market through referral, co-sell, subcontract, white-label, training, and retainer arrangements.

## Partner Types

| Partner type | Role | Typical motion |
|---|---|---|
| Sales / referral partner | Introduces qualified opportunities | Referral fee or success fee. |
| Consulting partner | Owns delivery relationship and subcontracts Phil | Subcontract or co-branded delivery. |
| Software / vendor partner | Aligns Velocity with platform implementation | Joint solution architecture and governance. |
| Training partner | Uses Academy content or certification pathway | Licensing, cohort delivery, or instructor-led training. |
| Subcontractor partner | Uses Phil as specialist architecture capacity | Day rate, sprint, or retainer. |

## Commercial Motions

- referral
- co-sell
- subcontract
- white-label delivery
- co-branded delivery
- advisory retainer
- training/license
- tool enablement

## Client Ownership

Default:

- partner owns sales and account relationship
- partner controls proposal and contracting
- Phil / ZenCloud provides specialist capability
- Velocity can be internal, co-branded, or client-visible depending on agreement

Exceptions require explicit agreement.

## Proposal Ownership

Partner-owned proposals should define:

- problem statement
- scope
- role of Phil / ZenCloud
- deliverables
- data handling
- AI/tool boundaries
- acceptance process
- commercial model
- liability and IP placeholders

## Liability Placeholders

Every channel model requires legal review for:

- liability cap
- indemnity
- professional advice disclaimer
- data processing
- subcontractor disclosure
- AI use
- IP rights
- confidentiality
- insurance

## Enablement Assets

Partners should receive:

- partner briefing pack
- sales cheat sheet
- qualification checklist
- commercial offer sheet
- demo flow
- delivery capacity model
- white-label rules
- AI and client data policy

## Partner Qualification

Good partners:

- sell advisory or transformation work
- have client trust
- need architecture depth
- respect IP and confidentiality
- understand governance
- accept human review of AI outputs
- can provide access to decision makers

Poor-fit partners:

- want free speculative architecture
- push unsupported AI claims
- cannot protect client data
- bypass governance
- expect unlimited delivery capacity

## Success Measures

- qualified opportunities
- conversion to paid discovery/sprint
- repeatable delivery cadence
- low rework
- partner satisfaction
- client decision speed
- reusable artefacts
- clear IP boundaries
