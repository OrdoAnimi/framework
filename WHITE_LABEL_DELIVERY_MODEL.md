# White-Label Delivery Model

## Purpose

This document defines how Velocity-aligned architecture work can be delivered under partner branding while preserving Phil / ZenCloud intellectual property, quality controls, and client data boundaries.

## Delivery Modes

| Mode | Client sees | Use when |
|---|---|---|
| White-label | Partner brand only | Partner owns the relationship and wants a seamless client experience. |
| Co-branded | Partner plus Phil / ZenCloud | Partner wants specialist credibility visible. |
| Named subcontractor | Partner prime, Phil named | Procurement or transparency requires subcontractor disclosure. |

## What Can Be Rebadged

The following can usually be rebadged:

- workshop agendas
- discovery questions
- architecture decision logs
- maturity assessments
- executive packs
- solution summaries
- governance models
- PMO alignment artefacts
- status/reporting templates
- handover packs

## What Remains Internal

Keep internal unless agreed:

- Velocity internal method notes
- Hermes routing logic
- prompt libraries
- proprietary templates
- StudioSix production workflows
- internal QA checklists
- commercial pricing logic
- reusable research notes

## Template Layers

Use three layers:

1. Internal Velocity structure: decision-first logic and artefact model.
2. Partner delivery template: partner logo, tone, document standards, and naming.
3. Client artefact: client-safe content, approved data, and final issue controls.

## Naming Neutralisation

When white-labelled, replace:

- Velocity Architecture Framework -> architecture decision framework
- VAF-SA -> solution architecture method
- StudioSix -> delivery production support
- Hermes -> internal intake/routing process
- EA Artefact Generator -> artefact production workflow
- PMO Portal -> delivery workspace or engagement workspace

## IP Boundaries

Partner/client can own:

- client-specific findings
- client-specific artefacts
- approved deliverables
- decision records created for the engagement

Phil / ZenCloud retains:

- generic frameworks
- reusable methods
- templates not sold outright
- prompts and tool logic
- training material
- research and publishing IP
- internal operating model

## Confidentiality Rules

- Do not publish client examples without written approval.
- Do not commit client artefacts to public repos.
- Do not use client names in demos unless authorised.
- Use sanitised or fictional examples in public material.
- Remove metadata that exposes client systems, people, contracts, or risks.

## AI and Tool Use

- Public AI demos use synthetic data.
- Real client data requires approved environment.
- AI outputs are drafts requiring human review.
- Partner/client policies override default tool use.
- Prompts and generated outputs must be handled as engagement records where they include client context.

## QA Checklist

Before issue:

- correct branding mode selected
- partner template applied
- no internal Velocity labels unless approved
- no confidential data leaked
- no unsupported production-readiness claims
- AI output reviewed by practitioner
- decisions and assumptions traceable
- artefact owner and version visible
- storage location approved
