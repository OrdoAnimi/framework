## Executive Summary

Most large organisations do not have an artefact problem. They have a decision-flow problem.

Architecture reviews produce decks. Governance gates produce sign-offs. Programs produce status reports. Decisions — the actual choices that determine cost, risk, and delivery outcome — are buried in email threads, lost in workshops, or never made explicit at all.

The Velocity Architecture Framework (VAF) addresses this directly.

Velocity compresses the working patterns of enterprise architecture, business architecture, solution architecture, and program governance into one decision-first operating model, operated by a qualified practitioner using AI-assisted tools.

This document provides the executive framing for enterprise and consultancy partner conversations.

---

## The Problem: Artefact-First Architecture

### What happens in most organisations

Architecture functions are measured by what they produce:
- Architecture principles documents
- Current-state diagrams
- Future-state diagrams
- Reference architectures
- Standards registers

These artefacts are necessary. They are not sufficient.

The failure mode is well understood:

> The project team finishes its architecture review. An architecture document is produced. It is reviewed, approved, and filed. Six months later, the program has diverged from the documented design. No one can explain why. The decisions that caused the divergence were never recorded.

This is the artefact trap: governance measures the document, not the decision.

### The consequences

- Architectural drift is invisible until it is expensive
- Trade-off decisions are undocumented and unenforceable
- Onboarding new architects requires re-discovering prior reasoning
- Architecture reviews become compliance theatre rather than decision support
- SLT cannot interrogate the decision trail behind a major program

---

## The Velocity Thesis: Decision-First, Artefact-Second

Velocity inverts the conventional sequence.

**Conventional:** Design → Document → Govern → Deliver  
**Velocity:** Decide → Record → Govern → Artefact

In the Velocity model:

1. Every architecture engagement begins with a decision question — not a diagram
2. Options are evaluated against defined criteria — business value, data readiness, security risk, AI readiness
3. The decision is recorded in a structured Decision Record before any artefact is produced
4. The artefact is derived from the decision — not the other way around
5. Governance confirms the decision logic — not just the document format

The result is an architecture function that is traceable, auditable, and aligned to business outcomes.

## Why This Matters to SLT

Senior leaders are not served by architecture documents. They are served by:

- **Decision clarity:** What was decided, when, by whom, and on what basis
- **Risk visibility:** What risk was accepted, and what was mitigated
- **Investment confidence:** Whether the program is proceeding on sound architectural grounds
- **Delivery alignment:** Whether the delivery team is executing what was decided

Velocity produces these outcomes directly, through a structured operating model that generates traceable, executive-readable outputs at every stage of an engagement.

## The Four-Box Operating Model

Velocity integrates four domains into a single decision-first flow:

```
┌─────────────────────────┬─────────────────────────┐
│   Enterprise            │   Business              │
│   Architecture          │   Architecture          │
│                         │                         │
│   Strategic alignment   │   Capabilities, value   │
│   Platform governance   │   streams, operating    │
│   Standards & patterns  │   model, benefits       │
├─────────────────────────┼─────────────────────────┤
│   Solution              │   PMO / Program         │
│   Architecture          │   Delivery              │
│                         │                         │
│   Design decisions      │   Governance cadence    │
│   Trade-off analysis    │   Risk & assumptions    │
│   Integration patterns  │   Decision registers    │
└─────────────────────────┴─────────────────────────┘
```

Each box is not a separate silo. In Velocity, they share:

- A common decision vocabulary
- A shared artefact set
- A single traceability thread from business outcome to delivery milestone

---

## Cross-Cutting Lenses

Five lenses are applied across all four boxes at every architecture decision point:

| Lens | What it evaluates |
|------|-------------------|
| **Business Value** | Does this decision advance a defined business outcome? |
| **Data Readiness** | Is the data required to execute this decision available, clean, and governed? |
| **Security Risk** | What security controls are required? What risk is being accepted? |
| **AI Readiness** | Is the organisation and its data prepared to use AI in this context? |
| **Governance & Traceability** | Can this decision be audited, challenged, and updated as context changes? |

These lenses are not compliance checkboxes. They are structured evaluation prompts applied at each decision gate.

## How a Certified Practitioner Uses the Framework

A Velocity-certified practitioner:

1. Enters an engagement with a defined decision framework, not a blank canvas
2. Applies the four-box operating model to scope the engagement
3. Uses the five lenses to structure evaluation
4. Produces a decision register before any architecture diagram is drawn
5. Generates board-ready artefacts using VAF Agentic AI
6. Reviews, validates, and signs off every output as the accountable practitioner

Certification is structured across two tracks:

- **Foundation:** Decision-first principles, artefact vocabulary, VAF operating model
- **Practitioner:** Full engagement delivery, board pack production, tool usage, governance facilitation

## Pilot Engagement Proposal

- Output: Architecture Board Pack, Decision Register, ADR set, Risk and Assumptions Register
- Method: Decision-first sprint using VAF operating model and Agentic AI tooling
- Review: Practitioner-led board presentation at conclusion

**Pilot conditions:**
- Client provides a defined architecture question or program scope
- Client data used in engagement is synthetic or appropriately sanitised (refer to AI and Data Usage Policy)
- All outputs are practitioner-reviewed before delivery
- Pilot results in a concrete deliverable — not a report on what could be done
