# 5.6 — The Architecture of the Next Decade

The decision system is complete. The harder question is what feeds it.

This article closes the implementation arc and opens the next architectural question:
not how decisions are routed, but how organisations learn to recognise which questions matter before they become delivery crises.

The article introduces:

- The generative layer
- Environmental sensing
- Anticipatory architecture
- Signal interpretation
- Baseline legibility
- Decision-system boundaries

The decision system routes decisions well. The next challenge is recognising the signal before the crisis arrives.
