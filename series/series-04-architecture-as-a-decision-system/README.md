# Series 4 — Architecture as a Decision System

Six articles building a unified model of architecture as a decision system. Published 2025.

The fundamental failure of enterprise architecture is not poor documentation, weak governance, or inadequate tooling. It is the absence of a decision system — the structural conditions under which architectural intent governs behaviour, authority is exercised and recorded, and the organisation learns from the gap between intent and execution.

Three structural elements form a closed loop:

- **Constraint clarity** — architectural intent expressed as constraints that shape behaviour at the point of decision, not principles consulted after the fact
- **Authority design** — the deliberate specification of who can make which decisions, under what conditions, with what consequences for deviation
- **Deviation visibility** — the feedback loop that keeps the decision system calibrated to reality
