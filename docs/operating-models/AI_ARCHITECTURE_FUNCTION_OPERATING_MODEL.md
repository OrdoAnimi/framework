# AI Architecture Function Operating Model

**Status:** Active estate operating doctrine  
**Authority:** Founder / Chief AI Architect  
**Estate governor:** Magister Automatorum  
**Professional method:** OrdoAnimi  
**Delivery and productisation:** ## 1. Purpose

This operating model applies the duties of a senior enterprise AI Architect across the OrdoAnimi, Velocity, and Ordo Animi estate.

It is not a personal job-description exercise. It defines the organisational function required to turn the estate from a founder-led collection of AI-assisted products, frameworks, tools, and experiments into a coherent AI company with clear technical authority, specialist responsibilities, production standards, and evidence.

## 2. Founder's role

The Founder acts as **Chief AI Architect and Design Authority**.

The Founder is accountable for:

- enterprise and product architecture coherence;
- translation of business intent into technical direction;
- reference architectures and approved patterns;
- architecture decisions and exceptions;
- AI use-case, risk, autonomy, and data classification;
- model, platform, and build-versus-buy decisions;
- cross-domain technical governance;
- review and acceptance of specialist engineering evidence;
- customer and executive architecture communication;
- prioritisation of architectural debt and capability investment;
- final technical recommendation to the Founder/CEO decision surface.

The Founder is not required to be the deepest practitioner in every specialist domain. The function requires sufficient technical depth to challenge designs, understand evidence, identify risk, make cross-domain trade-offs, and hold specialists accountable.

The Founder must not personally absorb the specialist accountabilities of ML engineering, data engineering, platform engineering, security engineering, SRE, frontend engineering, or product management merely because the current estate is founder-operated.

## 3. Operating principle

```text
Founder intent
  -> portfolio and product qualification
  -> architecture and risk classification
  -> specialist engineering contracts
  -> controlled implementation
  -> evaluation and assurance
  -> production authorisation
  -> operational evidence
  -> portfolio learning and adaptation
```

The Chief AI Architect owns the coherence of this chain. Specialist functions own the depth of their work within it.

## 4. AI architecture organisation

### 4.1 Chief AI Architecture and Design Authority

**Authority:** Founder / OrdoAnimi

Owns the enterprise AI reference architecture, architecture principles, decision records, technical standards, design reviews, exception decisions, and cross-estate architecture roadmap.

### 4.2 Product, customer, and value architecture

**Authorities:** Imperium, Mercatus, Forma

Owns user problems, product outcomes, portfolio choices, service design, customer journeys, commercial hypotheses, product lifecycle, and release value.

This function determines why a capability should exist. It does not choose technical implementation without architecture review.

### 4.3 AI application and agent engineering

**Authorities:** Ordo Animi, Velocity agentic platform, product repositories

Owns application services, agent workflows, tool integrations, state handling, prompt implementation, API contracts, deterministic control logic, testing, and code quality.

### 4.4 AI data and retrieval engineering

**Authorities:** Memoria, Fabrica, Nexus, product data workstreams

Owns ingestion, transformation, schemas, lineage, embeddings, indexes, retrieval, data quality, access filtering, tenancy, retention, deletion, and external data transfers.

### 4.5 Cloud platform and infrastructure engineering

**Authority:** Praesidium

Owns cloud landing patterns, networking, identity, secrets, containers, serverless services, managed AI platforms, infrastructure as code, CI/CD, supply-chain security, resilience, rollback, and platform cost controls.

### 4.6 AI security, privacy, and policy engineering

**Authorities:** Lex and Praesidium

Lex defines applicable policy, privacy, jurisdiction, contractual, and processing requirements. Praesidium converts those requirements into implementable security and platform controls.

### 4.7 Evaluation and assurance engineering

**Authority:** Probatio

Owns golden datasets, evaluation methods, benchmark results, test calibration, safety tests, prompt-injection tests, release evidence, control effectiveness, exceptions, and assurance reports.

Probatio is independent from the team that generates the output being assessed.

### 4.8 Observability and AI operations

**Authority:** Specula

Owns runtime traces, logs, metrics, prompt/model/tool/retrieval version telemetry, quality drift, cost thresholds, incident detection, operational dashboards, alerting, and service review evidence.

### 4.9 Identity, authority, and runtime context

**Authorities:** Civitas and Memoria

Civitas owns people, organisations, roles, tenants, engagements, permissions, and accountable authority. Memoria owns approved, consented, expiring runtime context and its boundary from permanent evidence.

### 4.10 Evidence, archive, and client presentation

**Authorities:** Archivum and Arcadium

Archivum preserves accepted artefacts, decisions, evaluation evidence, release records, residual risks, and engagement history. Arcadium provides authorised presentation without assuming archive authority.

### 4.11 External integration

**Authority:** Nexus

Owns adapters, canonical transfer objects, integration contracts, external-system writes, transfer logs, retries, and failure handling.

### 4.12 Experience and design system

**Authority:** Forma

Owns design tokens, shared interaction patterns, accessibility, public/private presentation rules, content patterns, and user-experience consistency across products.

## 6. Architecture review gates

### Gate 0 — Portfolio qualification

Imperium determines whether the idea should remain a lab, become a shared capability, enter product discovery, or stop.

No new repository is created merely because an idea can be implemented.

### Gate 1 — Product and use-case qualification

defines the user, problem, value hypothesis, non-goals, success measures, and operating context.

### Gate 2 — AI and risk classification

Velocity and Lex classify AI necessity, autonomy, consequence, customer exposure, data sensitivity, jurisdiction, and required human authority.

### Gate 3 — Architecture approval

The Chief AI Architect approves the end-to-end architecture, model/platform decision, data boundary, agent pattern, integration contracts, security model, and operational ownership.

### Gate 4 — Engineering readiness

Specialist teams confirm repositories, APIs, schemas, environments, IaC, test plan, evaluation plan, telemetry, and acceptance criteria.

### Gate 5 — Build and integration

Engineering implements against the approved contract. Material design deviations require a new decision rather than silent code drift.

### Gate 6 — Evaluation and assurance

Probatio and Praesidium assess deterministic tests, model evaluations, security cases, failure behaviour, latency, cost, and human-review outcomes.

### Gate 7 — Production authorisation

The Chief AI Architect approves or rejects release based on engineering, assurance, operational, and residual-risk evidence.

### Gate 8 — Operational review

Specula supplies quality, reliability, cost, security, and user-outcome evidence. Material drift triggers reassessment, remediation, replacement, or retirement.

## 7. Current estate assessment

The estate is architecturally advanced but operationally uneven.

### Existing strengths

- coherent institutional decomposition across governance, policy, architecture, product, identity, memory, generation, assurance, archive, integration, security, observability, experience, and commercialisation;
- strong architecture and decision-governance doctrine;
- working TypeScript and React applications across multiple product surfaces;
- agentic architecture prototypes using model APIs, specialised agents, structured prompts, validation, and GitHub actions;
- established cloud, security, delivery, and stakeholder leadership experience;
- strong human-accountability, data-boundary, and client-isolation principles;
- significant repository and deployment evidence;
- emerging control plane through Magister and Obsidian.

### Principal gaps

1. **No single approved enterprise AI reference architecture.** The institutions exist, but their production interaction is not yet represented as one implementable target architecture.
2. **Architecture and constitutional text sometimes conflict with working product reality.** Repository boundaries and maturity language require reconciliation.
3. **Evaluation is not yet an estate-wide engineering discipline.** Validation exists, but versioned datasets, benchmarks, regression thresholds, and CI gates are incomplete.
4. **Agent and prompt management is not yet centralised.** Prompts, model choices, tools, and permissions are not consistently registered and versioned across products.
5. **RAG and vector infrastructure are not yet demonstrated as a shared production capability.**
6. **Managed AI platform usage is not standardised.** Direct APIs, cloud services, and future local models require approved selection patterns.
7. **Infrastructure as code and GitOps are inconsistent.** The estate cannot yet be reproduced or promoted through one standard deployment contract.
8. **AI observability is mostly conceptual.** End-to-end traces, quality drift, cost telemetry, and operational alerts are not consistently implemented.
9. **Security controls are stronger in doctrine than execution evidence.** Prompt injection, tool abuse, data leakage, and denial-of-wallet testing require implementation.
10. **Product lifecycle and support boundaries remain unclear for several repositories.** Labs, tools, platforms, shared services, and products are not always distinguished.
11. **Engineering ownership is currently concentrated in the Founder and AI coding tools.** Specialist responsibilities and review independence need to be modelled even before human teams are hired.
12. **Commercial proof remains limited.** The estate needs bounded reference use cases, case studies, support terms, and measurable customer outcomes before broad product claims.

## 8. Gap-closing programme

### Release A — Establish authority and target architecture

**Outcome:** One governed AI estate rather than independent repositories.

Required actions:

- formally record the Founder as Chief AI Architect and Design Authority;
- approve this operating model through Magister;
- publish the enterprise AI reference architecture;
- classify all repositories as governance, method, shared platform, product, delivery tool, academy, lab, archive candidate, or retired;
- nominate one product proving ground: Velocity Agentic Architect;
- freeze creation of new AI repositories unless Gate 0 is passed;
- resolve active identity and boundary contradictions;
- establish an AI architecture decision register and exception process.

### Release B — Make quality and security executable

**Outcome:** AI outputs are measured and release claims are evidenced.

Required actions:

- create Probatio's evaluation standard and initial golden dataset;
- establish prompt, model, retrieval, tool, and configuration registries;
- create the AI system card and threat model for the Velocity agent;
- implement deterministic tests, model evaluations, injection tests, tool-permission tests, and data-leakage tests;
- store evaluation and assurance results with releases;
- prohibit production-ready claims without passing evidence.

### Release C — Establish the shared AI platform pattern

**Outcome:** A production-capable reference architecture exists.

Required actions:

- implement one controlled RAG and retrieval path;
- adopt Azure as the primary reference platform unless an ADR selects otherwise;
- define identity, secrets, networking, model access, search, storage, and logging patterns;
- create Terraform-based environment provisioning;
- establish CI/CD, evaluation, security, promotion, rollback, and teardown workflows;
- define provider fallback and exit patterns.

### Release D — Establish operations and economics

**Outcome:** The estate can operate AI systems rather than merely build them.

Required actions:

- implement Specula telemetry for model, prompt, retrieval, tools, latency, cost, quality, failures, and escalation;
- define service objectives and support ownership;
- create incident, provider outage, data deletion, rollback, and retirement runbooks;
- implement AI FinOps measures including cost per successful outcome;
- run an operational review and record the residual risks.

### Release E — Scale teams and products

**Outcome:** The Founder leads a repeatable AI company rather than personally carrying every function.

Required actions:

- define role scorecards for AI application engineering, AI data engineering, platform engineering, evaluation engineering, AI security, and AI operations;
- use contractors or specialist partners where depth is required before permanent hiring;
- route all work through architecture-approved engineering work orders;
- require independent Probatio and Praesidium review for material releases;
- publish a reference case demonstrating strategy-to-production delivery;
- promote shared patterns into Velocity Academy for team onboarding and customer capability transfer.

## 9. Founder skill boundary and development priorities

The Founder does not need expert implementation depth in every specialist field. The Founder requires:

### Must remain Lead

- enterprise and solution architecture;
- design authority and architecture governance;
- cloud, security, delivery, and operating-model decisions;
- customer and executive communication;
- product-to-technology translation;
- cross-domain risk and trade-off judgement;
- technical team leadership and mentoring.

### Must reach or maintain Working depth

- agentic architecture and tool-control patterns;
- AI evaluation concepts and evidence interpretation;
- RAG and AI data architecture;
- managed AI platform patterns;
- API, event, identity, and integration architecture;
- infrastructure, observability, and AI FinOps design;
- prompt and agent security;
- ability to inspect, challenge, and diagnose implementation with engineering teams.

### May remain specialist literacy

- foundation-model training;
- advanced ML research;
- GPU kernel optimisation;
- low-level inference-server tuning;
- deep data-science experimentation;
- language-specific coding speed.

The Founder must know when a specialist is required, how to define the work, what evidence to request, how to identify weak engineering, and when to reject a design.

## 10. Team simulation before hiring

Until human specialist teams exist, the estate will simulate organisational separation through repositories, work orders, and independent review prompts.

- ChatGPT operates as strategy, product, architecture, research, planning, and design-authority support.
- Claude operates as the implementation squad within explicit work orders.
- Probatio review is executed separately from generation.
- Praesidium review is executed separately from implementation.
- Specula requirements are defined before deployment.
- GitHub holds implementation and evidence.
- Obsidian holds relationship memory, synthesis, and operating continuity.

AI tools may perform multiple functions, but the artefacts, prompts, and review sessions must preserve separation of duties.

## 11. Immediate decisions

1. Adopt **Chief AI Architect and Design Authority** as the Founder's internal operating role.
2. Treat the senior AI Architect market specification as the target organisational capability, not a personal job checklist.
3. Make Velocity Agentic Architect the reference implementation for closing engineering and assurance gaps.
4. Establish Probatio, Praesidium, and Specula as operating review functions rather than constitutional placeholders.
5. Freeze unnecessary repository creation and move learning into bounded work packages.
6. Require architecture, evaluation, security, operational, and cost evidence before any AI product is described as production-ready.
7. Use as the product and delivery surface through which the completed operating model becomes a client-facing capability.

## 12. Success condition

The AI architecture function is operating when the Founder can direct specialist teams through a coherent architecture; every team knows its authority and required evidence; releases pass independent quality, security, and operational gates; and the estate can demonstrate one end-to-end AI system that is valuable, secure, observable, supportable, cost-aware, and recoverable.
