# AI FinRev DevOps Model

## Status

Internal doctrine. Working title and operating model.

## Purpose

AI FinRev DevOps connects four control concerns that are often separated during AI adoption:

- FinOps: cost, token, infrastructure, and vendor control
- RevOps: commercial signal, pipeline, buyer value, and measurable outcomes
- DevOps: build, test, deployment, monitoring, and operational flow
- Governance: ownership, risk, evidence, human review, and decision traceability

## Problem

AI estates can expand faster than organisations can govern them. New models, copilots, agents, APIs, workflows, subscriptions, and experiments create sprawl across business units and delivery teams.

Without a joined operating model, organisations experience:

- duplicated subscriptions
- uncontrolled token and infrastructure costs
- pilots with no production path
- disconnected commercial goals
- weak human accountability
- overlapping tools
- vendor-led architecture decisions
- unclear data boundaries
- poor monitoring and retirement controls

## Core Principle

Every AI capability should be traceable from business value to architecture decision, resource cost, deployment state, risk control, and operational owner.

## Operating Flow

1. Capture the business outcome.
2. Classify the workload and data.
3. Select the cheapest capable model or tool.
4. Define architecture, control, and human-review boundaries.
5. Build and deploy through controlled delivery paths.
6. Measure technical performance, cost, adoption, and commercial value.
7. Continue, change, scale, or retire based on evidence.

## Control Questions

### FinOps

- What is the expected token and infrastructure cost?
- Which free-tier or subscription limits apply?
- Are multiple tools performing the same function?
- Is a cheaper model sufficient for the workload?

### RevOps

- What buyer, user, or business outcome is supported?
- Does the capability create revenue, reduce cost, reduce risk, or improve throughput?
- Which campaign, pipeline, or client signal supports continued investment?

### DevOps

- How is the capability built, tested, deployed, monitored, rolled back, and retired?
- Which repository and environment are authoritative?
- What changes require approval?

### Governance

- Who owns the decision and output?
- What data can the system access?
- What human review is mandatory?
- What evidence is retained?

## Workload Routing Principle

Use the cheapest capable execution path.

- light planning and synthesis: ChatGPT
- bounded medium implementation: Codex or equivalent execution agent
- long-form research and writing: Claude Portal or equivalent premium reasoning surface
- heavy multi-repo execution: Claude Console or equivalent heavy execution surface
- commodity classification and summarisation: evaluate lower-cost or local models

The tool names may change. The routing principle should remain stable.

## Measures

- cost per useful output
- cost per deployed use case
- time from idea to governed pilot
- time from pilot to operational capability
- percentage of AI use cases with named owners
- percentage with documented data boundaries
- percentage with human-review controls
- duplicated tool spend
- realised business value
- retirement rate for low-value experiments

## Velocity Fit

Velocity contributes the decision and traceability model. AI FinRev DevOps extends that model across economics, revenue, engineering flow, and governance.
