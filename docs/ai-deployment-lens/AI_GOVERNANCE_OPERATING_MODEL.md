# AI Governance Operating Model

## Status

Internal doctrine. Not yet a public launch claim.

## Purpose

Define how organisations can govern AI deployment across business value, architecture, data, tools, human accountability, agents, cost, risk, and operations.

## Core Thesis

AI deployment fails when adoption moves faster than ownership, architecture, data controls, cost governance, and operational accountability.

The lesson from cloud applies directly to AI: unmanaged adoption creates sprawl, duplicated spend, vendor-led decisions, weak traceability, and control gaps.

## Governance Layers

### 1. Business Decision

Every AI use case must have a business owner, intended outcome, value hypothesis, risk tolerance, and explicit statement of what remains human-controlled.

### 2. Architecture Decision

Every use case must be framed across enterprise, business, solution, data, security, and delivery architecture.

### 3. Data Boundary

Data must be classified before use. Public, internal, confidential, and restricted information require different tool and hosting boundaries.

### 4. Model and Tool Selection

Select the cheapest capable tool that meets security, privacy, quality, observability, and contractual requirements.

### 5. Human Review and Accountability

AI outputs are drafts unless an approved control model says otherwise. A named human remains accountable for review and use.

### 6. Agent and Workflow Control

Agents must operate within explicit permissions. Read, draft, write, publish, deploy, and send actions must be separately controlled.

### 7. AI FinOps

Track token use, subscriptions, free-tier limits, infrastructure cost, vendor overlap, and value realised.

### 8. Risk, Security, and Compliance

Document risk rating, required controls, approval evidence, monitoring, incident response, and shutoff mechanisms.

### 9. Delivery and Operations

Define how the use case moves from idea to pilot to production, including support ownership, monitoring, review cadence, and retirement criteria.

## Decision Gates

1. Idea intake
2. Architecture framing
3. Data and security boundary
4. Pilot approval
5. Deployment approval
6. Operational review

## Minimum Artefacts

- AI use case canvas
- AI architecture decision record
- data classification and boundary note
- model and tool selection note
- human review and accountability model
- AI risk and control register
- AI FinOps estimate
- pilot success criteria
- deployment readiness checklist
- operational review record

## Velocity Fit

Velocity should treat AI governance as decision traceability, not documentation volume.

The core questions are:

- What decision is being made?
- Who owns it?
- What data is involved?
- What tool or model is being used?
- What human review is required?
- What risk is accepted?
- What cost is justified?
- How does the pilot become operational capability?

## Boundary

This model does not claim expertise in foundation model training, deep ML research, model weight optimisation, or specialist GPU engineering.
