## One-Page Overview

**ZenCloud advises. StudioSix produces. Velocity decides.**

The Velocity ecosystem is three things operating as one:

1. **ZenCloud** — the advisory and consulting practice that delivers engagements to enterprise clients and operates through consultancy partners
2. **Velocity Architecture Framework** — the decision-first operating model, academy, and certification that powers every engagement
3. **StudioSix** — the product studio, media production, publishing, and labs that converts advisory thinking into scalable content, tools, and products

These three are not separate businesses. They are roles within one integrated operation, run by Phil Myint as Principal Architect and founder.

│                         │                         │
│   Design decisions      │   Governance cadence    │
│   Trade-off analysis    │   Risk & assumptions    │

All four boxes operate under the same decision-first model. A Velocity practitioner moves between boxes as the engagement requires — they are not siloed roles.

## Cross-Cutting Lenses

Five lenses are applied across all four boxes at every decision point:

| Lens | Question it asks |
|------|-----------------|
| **Business Value** | Does this decision advance a defined business outcome? |
| **Data Readiness** | Is the data available, clean, and governed to execute this decision? |
| **Security Risk** | What risk is being accepted and what controls are required? |
| **AI Readiness** | Is the organisation prepared to apply AI in this context? |
| **Governance & Traceability** | Can this decision be audited, challenged, and updated? |

## Labs / Trading Boundary

StudioSix Labs is Phil's personal research boundary.

- Trading systems, financial models, and experimental tools live here
- Clearly labelled as personal research — not part of ZenCloud advisory or Velocity Framework
- Content produced from Labs research carries appropriate disclaimers
- Labs output is never presented to enterprise clients or co-branded with ZenCloud

The Labs boundary is a governance decision, not a technical one. It protects ZenCloud's professional reputation and ensures client data boundaries are maintained.

## Solo Delivery Model

ZenCloud currently operates as a solo practice with Phil Myint as Principal Architect.

Solo delivery is viable for:

- Decision sprints (4 weeks)
- Advisory overlay (rolling retainer)
- Board pack and artefact production
- Architecture governance reset (6–12 weeks)
- Capability uplift (training delivery)
- Research and framework development

Solo delivery is constrained for:

- Simultaneous multi-client delivery
- Engagements requiring on-site presence across multiple locations
- Large program embedded advisory (>6 months) without partner support

---

## Subcontracting Model

When engagement volume exceeds solo capacity:

1. Velocity-certified practitioners can be engaged as associate delivery resources
2. The partner consultancy provides staffing within the engagement structure
3. ZenCloud provides oversight, artefact standards, and practitioner accountability
4. All subcontracted delivery operates under the same AI and Data Usage Policy

Subcontractors do not carry ZenCloud engagement accountability. Phil retains principal accountability for all delivered outputs.

## How Client Engagements Become Reusable IP

Every client engagement generates:

1. A decision register — anonymised and generalised for framework development
2. Patterns — recurring trade-offs that become template artefacts
3. Scenarios — anonymised case studies for certification content
4. Insights — generalised observations for Medium articles and research papers

Client-specific content remains client IP. Generalised patterns become Velocity Framework IP.

This is not extraction. It is the natural process of a practitioner who builds a framework from real engagement experience.

## Governance Model for Adding New Repos or Frameworks

Before adding a new repo or framework:

1. Does it fit an existing category in the Portfolio Repo Map?
2. Is it a cross-cutting lens on the parent Velocity model — or a new empire?
3. What is the naming convention?
4. Is it public or private?
5. Does it require a brand system update?
6. What is the minimum README standard?

New frameworks must be cross-cutting lenses or specialised extensions of the decision-first operating model — not separate, unrelated products.
