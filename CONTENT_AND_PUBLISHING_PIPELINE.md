## Publishing Model

StudioSix produces. ZenCloud advises. Velocity decides.

All content produced through StudioSix originates in ZenCloud advisory thinking and Velocity Framework development. The pipeline converts practitioner thinking into scalable content — articles, books, podcasts, courses, certifications, and tools.

No content is produced once. Every idea travels the full pipeline.

---

## Channel Roles

### Medium

**Role:** Long-form editorial — decision-first thinking, architecture commentary, practitioner insights

- Primary publication for ZenCloud / Velocity thought leadership
- Audience: architects, delivery leads, EA practitioners, technology executives
- Format: 1,000–3,000 words per article
- Cadence: 2–4 articles per month (target)
- Monetisation: Medium Partner Program (secondary) — primary value is audience and credibility

### LinkedIn

**Role:** Short-form discovery — practitioner insights, engagement observations, framework fragments

- Primary discovery channel — drives to Medium, Velocity Academy, and ZenCloud
- Audience: broader professional network including SLT and consultancy contacts
- Format: 150–400 words, one key idea per post
- Cadence: 3–5 posts per week (target)
- Not used for promotional content — used for thinking

### Velocity Library

**Role:** Curated reference — framework documentation, artefact templates, practitioner guides

- Hosted within the Velocity Architecture Framework site
- Access: some public, some gated (Velocity Academy membership)
- Format: structured Markdown and PDF documentation
- Updated: continuously as framework evolves

### StudioSix Media

**Role:** Production studio output — books, podcasts, YouTube series, presentations

- Brand under which all long-form content is published
- Distinct from ZenCloud advisory — creative and editorial voice
- Manages distribution relationships (Amazon KDP, Spotify, YouTube)

## Presentation and Keynote Pipeline

- A SlideShare or public PDF

- A potential podcast episode recording

## Repurposing Workflow

Every piece of content travels the following path (not every piece reaches every stage — the pipeline is a flow, not a mandate):

```
Idea
  ↓
LinkedIn post (150–400 words) — test engagement, refine framing
  ↓
Medium article (1,000–3,000 words) — expand with structure and depth
  ↓
StudioSix script (podcast/YouTube format)
  ↓
Podcast episode
  ↓
YouTube video (or short-form clip)
  ↓
Presentation / keynote slide deck
  ↓
Book chapter draft
  ↓
Course lesson (Velocity Academy)
  ↓
Certification module assessment question
  ↓
Tool prompt or template (VAF Agentic AI or artefact template)
```

Not every idea reaches the bottom of the pipeline. The pipeline is how good ideas get maximum value — not how every idea gets forced into a course.

## Public / Private Boundaries

### Public content

- All Velocity Framework documentation
- All Medium articles
- All LinkedIn posts
- All book content (on publication)
- All podcast episodes
- All YouTube content
- All research papers
- All presentations

### Private content (internal only)

- Engagement-specific analysis and working documents
- Client context and case material (until anonymised and cleared)
- Trading research and StudioSix Labs output (until published with clear disclaimers)
- Book drafts and course content in pre-release development

### Trading and personal research

All StudioSix Labs content — including trading research, financial analysis, and experimental products — is:

- Published separately under the StudioSix Labs label
- Never co-branded with ZenCloud advisory
- Always accompanied by a disclaimer: "This is personal research. It is not financial advice."
- Never used in enterprise client contexts or presentations

The trading boundary is a professional governance decision. Personal research has value. It must not create confusion about the professional advisory standing of ZenCloud.
