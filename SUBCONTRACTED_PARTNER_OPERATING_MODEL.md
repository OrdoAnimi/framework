# Subcontracted Partner Operating Model

## Purpose

This model defines how Phil / ZenCloud can deliver Velocity-aligned architecture work through a business consultancy partner where the partner owns the client relationship and Phil operates as part of the partner delivery team.

## Operating Chain

```text
Client -> Consultancy Partner -> Phil / ZenCloud
```

The client may experience Phil as a specialist architect, principal consultant, solution architect, or delivery architecture lead within the partner engagement. Velocity can remain internal unless the partner agrees to expose it.

## Role Model

| Party | Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|---|
| Client | Business owner and decision sponsor | Provides mandate, access, context, constraints, decisions, and approvals. |
| Consultancy partner | Account owner and commercial prime | Owns sales, proposal, commercial terms, client relationship, delivery governance, billing, and final client-facing commitments. |
| Phil / ZenCloud | Architecture execution specialist | Performs architecture diagnosis, decision facilitation, artefact shaping, governance design, and delivery architecture support. |
| StudioSix | Behind-the-scenes production studio | Supports artefact production, media/training assets, digital tools, templates, and research outputs. |
| Velocity | Internal operating model | Provides decision-first structure, four-box coordination, maturity diagnostics, and artefact logic. |

## Client-Facing Posture

Use partner-aligned role language:

- Principal Architect
- Enterprise Architecture Advisor
- Solution Architecture Lead
- Delivery Architecture Specialist
- Architecture Governance Advisor

Avoid overexposing internal product language unless approved:

- Do not lead with "Velocity" if the partner is white-labelling the engagement.
- Do not position StudioSix as a separate competing delivery brand.
- Do not imply Phil owns the client relationship when the partner is prime.

## Partner-Owned Areas

The partner owns:

- sales qualification
- statement of work
- commercials and billing
- client account governance
- legal terms
- liability model
- final proposal language
- client communication cadence
- client escalation pathway
- final document branding, unless co-branding is agreed

## Phil / ZenCloud-Owned Areas

Phil owns or leads:

- architecture decision diagnosis
- Velocity decision-flow analysis
- architecture coordination model
- workshop facilitation content
- VAF/VAF-SA method application
- architecture and delivery artefact structure
- executive narrative around decisions, risk, governance, and traceability
- internal quality review of architecture outputs

## StudioSix Support

StudioSix may support:

- template production
- presentation preparation
- diagram and visual language
- publication-ready artefacts
- training assets
- internal tool workflows
- PMO Portal / EA Artefact Generator demo assets

StudioSix is normally not presented as the client-facing prime unless agreed.

## Velocity as Internal Method

Velocity can remain an internal execution system:

- four-box decision model
- maturity assessment
- architecture coordination diagnostics
- reusable artefact patterns
- Hermes routing logic
- decision metrics

The partner can rebadge outputs under its own method while preserving Velocity structure internally.

## Commercial Placeholders

Commercial terms must be agreed per partner:

- day rate or fixed fee
- referral fee
- subcontract margin
- retainer model
- IP usage rights
- tool usage rights
- cancellation and rescheduling
- expenses
- payment timing

No pricing is defined in this document.

## Confidentiality and Data Handling

- Treat client data as partner/client confidential.
- Do not enter confidential data into public tools.
- Do not commit client material to public repos.
- Use synthetic or sanitised examples for demos.
- Use partner-approved document stores for client artefacts.
- AI outputs are drafts and require human review.

## Delivery Cadence

Recommended cadence:

- weekly partner check-in
- client workshop or playback as agreed
- internal architecture review before client issue
- decision log update after every major interaction
- artefact register update weekly
- escalation within 24 hours for scope, risk, data, or commercial issues

## Escalation Triggers

Escalate to the partner when:

- scope expands materially
- client asks for direct contracting
- confidential data handling is unclear
- AI/tool use is challenged
- deliverable ownership is ambiguous
- stakeholder access is blocked
- decision authority is missing
- commercial promises are requested outside the SOW

## Success Conditions

The model is working when:

- the partner retains client ownership
- Phil delivers specialist architecture value
- client-facing outputs are partner-aligned
- Velocity improves decision speed and traceability
- no client data enters public tools or repos
- delivery cadence and responsibilities are clear
