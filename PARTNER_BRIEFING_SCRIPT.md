## How to Use This Script

This is a talk track, not a word-for-word script. Read it before the meeting. Know the structure. Adapt the language to the room.

**Format:** Partner conversation — in-person or video  
**Audience:** Practice leads, advisory directors, architecture capability heads  
**Time:** 30 minutes talk, 30 minutes discussion  
**Supporting material:** `PARTNER_BRIEFING_PACK_OUTLINE.md` (slide deck outline for StudioSix production)

**Before you start:** Know the answer to these three questions about the partner:

1. What does their architecture advisory offering look like today — do they have one?
2. Who are their current enterprise clients and what kind of programs are active?
3. Are they in a growth phase, a cost-reduction phase, or a capability-building phase?

The script adapts depending on what you know. If you know nothing — ask in the first five minutes rather than guessing.

## Opening (Minutes 0–5)

### Positioning — Open with the shared problem, not with Velocity

Do not open with a product pitch. Open by naming something the partner already knows.

---

*"I want to start with something I suspect you have seen in your clients — because we have seen it consistently.*

*Architecture reviews happen. Documents get produced. Governance gates get cleared. And then, six or twelve months later, a program has diverged from its intended design — and nobody can explain why, because the decisions that caused the divergence were never recorded. They happened in workshops, in email threads, in informal conversations between a solution architect and a technical lead. But they were never made explicit.*

*The governance process approved the document. It never evaluated the decision.*

*That is the artefact trap — and it is the root cause of most architecture governance problems we see in enterprise organisations."*

---

**Pause. Let that land.**

If the partner nods, or volunteers an example from their own experience, follow it. That is your opening for the next section.

If they push back — "our clients don't have that problem" — ask: *"When a program diverges from its design, how does your client's architecture function know? And how do they trace the root cause?"* The answer almost always confirms the problem.

## The Velocity Thesis (Minutes 5–12)

### Name the inversion — decision-first, not artefact-first

---

*"Velocity Architecture Framework is built on a single inversion.*

*The conventional sequence is: design, document, govern, deliver. You produce the design. You document it. You put it through governance. And then delivery executes it — or doesn't.*

*The Velocity sequence is: decide, record, govern, artefact. Every engagement begins with a defined decision question. Options are evaluated against structured criteria. The decision is recorded before any artefact is produced. The artefact is derived from the decision — not the other way around.*

*That sounds simple. It is not common practice. Most architecture functions produce artefacts first and extract decision rationale later — if at all."*

---

### Introduce the four-box model

---

*"The framework connects four domains that most organisations treat as separate functions: Enterprise Architecture, Business Architecture, Solution Architecture, and PMO — program delivery and governance.*

*In the Velocity model, these four domains share a common decision vocabulary, a shared artefact set, and a single traceability thread from business outcome to delivery milestone.*

*And at every decision point across all four domains, five cross-cutting lenses are applied: Business Value, Data Readiness, Security Risk, AI Readiness, and Governance and Traceability.*

*These lenses are not compliance checkboxes. They are structured evaluation prompts — the questions a practitioner asks at every decision gate to make sure the decision is being evaluated against the right criteria."*

---

**Check in:** *"Does this map to what you are seeing with your clients? Is governance speed an issue — or is it more about decision quality and traceability?"*

Use the answer to calibrate how much time you spend on the model versus the engagement options.

## How the Ecosystem Fits Together (Minutes 12–18)

### Introduce ZenCloud, Velocity, and StudioSix — and where the partner sits

This section explains the three-part ecosystem and where the partner fits. Keep it tight. This is context, not a pitch.

---

*"ZenCloud is the advisory practice. We engage enterprise clients and partner with consultancy firms to provide the architecture advisory branch.*

*Velocity Architecture Framework is the operating model — the method, the certification pathway, and the tool layer that every ZenCloud engagement runs on.*

*StudioSix is our production arm — books, training content, research, presentations. It operates behind the advisory layer. Your clients will not see StudioSix unless there is a specific content or training need.*

*The working line is: ZenCloud advises. StudioSix produces. Velocity decides.*

*Where does a consultancy partner fit in that model? The partner owns the client relationship and the commercial terms. ZenCloud provides the architecture advisory capability. We operate as the architecture branch of your practice — white-label or named, depending on what works for your clients."*

---

### White-label vs named — address it directly

---

*"On naming — we are flexible. If you have an established architecture brand and want to introduce this capability under your own practice, that works. ZenCloud and Velocity stay in the background. We operate as a specialist resource you have brought in.*

*If the client values knowing who is delivering the architecture advisory — and some enterprise clients do — we can be named. Co-branding is agreed per engagement.*

*The default is white-label unless you tell us otherwise."*

## The Engagement Offers (Minutes 18–25)

### Lead with the FastStart — it is the right entry point for most partner conversations

---

*"The way most of our partner engagements begin is with the Velocity Executive Architecture FastStart.*

*It is a structured advisory engagement — either five days compressed or two to four weeks — that helps a senior technology or transformation leader diagnose where decision-flow breaks down in their organisation, prioritise the interventions that will have the highest impact, and leave with a 90-day action plan.*

*The FastStart is not a readiness assessment. It ends with a prioritised, sequenced, and owned action plan — and a clear recommendation for what comes next.*

*The ideal entry point is a leader who is new to a role, under scrutiny for the value their architecture function is delivering, or about to commit to a major investment and needs to know whether their decision-making infrastructure is ready for it."*

---

### Then introduce the Decision Sprint as the natural follow-on

---

*"After the FastStart, the most common follow-on is an Architecture Decision Sprint.*

*A Decision Sprint takes the single highest-priority unresolved architecture decision identified in the FastStart — and resolves it in four weeks. The output is a board-ready Architecture Board Pack, a formal Architecture Decision Record, a Trade-off Matrix, and the first entry in a Decision Register.*

*That is a concrete deliverable — not a report on what could be done. The board or governance committee sees the output. The decision is made and recorded. And the client has experienced the Velocity model in their real context.*

*From there, the conversation about a longer advisory engagement or capability uplift is much easier — because the value is demonstrated, not promised."*

## AI and Client Data — Address It Before They Ask (Minutes 25–28)

### Name the boundary clearly and confidently

Most partners will ask about AI. Answer it before the question comes up.

---

*"I want to address AI directly — because it will come up, and the answer matters.*

*We use VAF Agentic AI — our own tool layer — to compress the time required to produce artefacts. Board packs, ADRs, trade-off matrices, risk registers. What would take a practitioner two days to draft from scratch, we can draft in hours — and then the practitioner reviews, validates, and owns the output before anything goes to the client.*

*That is the critical point: the AI generates drafts. The practitioner reviews and owns. No AI-generated content is delivered to a client without Phil's sign-off.*

*On data: no confidential client data enters any public AI tool. We operate under a formal AI and Client Data Usage Policy. All engagement outputs are client IP on delivery. The AI does not train on client data.*

*If a client has specific AI governance requirements — enterprise-configured tools only, no AI at all — we accommodate that. It is agreed at kick-off."*

## Suggested Questions to Ask the Partner (Minutes 28–30)

Use the remaining time to understand the partner's situation. Do not pitch further. Ask.

---

**On their current architecture capability:**

*"What does your architecture advisory offering look like today? Do you have practitioners embedded in client engagements — or is architecture more of a review-and-sign-off function?"*

*"When clients come to you with an architecture problem, what does that typically look like? Is it a program that has stalled, a governance question, or something more strategic?"*

**On their clients:**

*"Which of your current clients do you think has the most acute decision-flow problem right now — where governance is slowing them down or decisions are being made without traceability?"*

*"Is there a client that is under pressure to demonstrate the value of their architecture function — from the board, a transformation office, or a cost review?"*

**On the partnership model:**

*"How do you currently engage specialist capability — do you subcontract, bring in associates, or prefer a co-delivery model?"*

*"What would white-label delivery look like for you in practice — is that a conversation you have had with other specialist firms?"*

**On timing:**

*"Is there a program or engagement starting in the next one to three months where a FastStart or a Decision Sprint would fit?"*

---

## Closing — Next Steps (Minute 30)

One specific next step. Not a list of possibilities.

---

*"Here is what I would suggest as a next step.*

*Identify one client — ideally one you mentioned in this conversation — where you think the FastStart would be the right entry point. We can run a 30-minute scoping call to define the engagement and talk through how you would introduce it.*

*If it is easier to start with a Decision Sprint on an active program — same process. Give me the decision question and the governance deadline, and we can scope it.*

*What I do not want is for this conversation to end without a specific client in mind. Who are you thinking about?"*

---

**End on that question. Let them answer. Whatever they say is your lead.**

## Common Objections and Responses

**"Our clients already have architecture functions. We don't want to step on toes."**

*"Velocity supports existing architecture teams — it doesn't displace them. The FastStart works with the existing EA or SA function to establish a better operating model. The practitioner is there to give the team a structured method, not to take over their accountability."*

---

**"How is this different from what a McKinsey or Deloitte architecture team would do?"**

*"Large firms provide strategy at the top and delivery at the bottom. The architecture layer in the middle — decision-first, traceable, board-visible — is often thin or inconsistent. Velocity fills that gap specifically. And because it is practitioner-led and AI-assisted, the speed and cost model is different."*

---

**"We're not sure our clients are ready for AI in architecture work."**

*"The AI is invisible to the client unless you want it visible. What the client sees is a board pack, an ADR, and a trade-off matrix — produced faster and at higher quality than a manual process. The tool that helped produce it is a practitioner accountability question, not a client-facing one. We disclose AI usage at kick-off, but it does not change the output or the accountability model."*

---

**"Can you work under our brand completely?"**

*"Yes. White-label delivery is the default. Phil operates as a specialist you have engaged. The Velocity method is applied but presented under your practice's framework and document standards. ZenCloud and Velocity are not named in client-facing materials."*
